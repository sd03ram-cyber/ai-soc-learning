# AI Terms Glossary

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)** – broad field of making machines perform tasks that normally need human intelligence, like recognizing images, understanding language, or making decisions.

**ML (Machine Learning)** – a subset of AI where instead of hardcoding rules, the system learns patterns from data and improves with more examples.

**DL (Deep Learning)** – a subset of ML using multi-layered neural networks, good at complex tasks like image recognition and language understanding because it learns hierarchical features on its own.

**LLM (Large Language Model)** – a deep learning model trained on massive text data to understand and generate human language, like GPT or Claude.

**Prompt** – the input text you give an AI model to get a response; can be a question, instruction, or piece of context.

**Token** – a chunk of text (word or part of a word) that the model reads/generates one unit at a time; models have limits on how many tokens they can process at once.

**Context window** – the maximum amount of text (in tokens) a model can "see" at once, including both the prompt and its own generated response.

**Hallucination** – when an AI model confidently generates information that sounds correct but is actually false or made up.
